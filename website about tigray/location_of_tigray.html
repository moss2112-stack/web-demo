Location of Tigray

Tigray is situated in the northernmost part of Ethiopia. It shares borders with:

  • Eritrea to the north
  • Amhara Region to the south
  • Afar Region to the east
  • Sudan to the west

The region is known for its diverse geography, featuring highland plateaus, rugged mountains, and expansive lowland plains.

Key Geographic Features

  • Capital City: Mekelle, the administrative and economic hub of Tigray.
  • Major Rivers: The Tekez√© River, a vital waterway forming a natural boundary with Sudan.
  • Mountains: The Simien Mountains, famous for their breathtaking landscapes and unique biodiversity.
  • Climate: Semi-arid in the lowlands and temperate in the highlands.

Strategic Importance

Tigray's location has historically been a crossroads for trade, culture, and religion. It connects the Ethiopian highlands with the Red Sea, fostering cultural exchange and economic activity. Its proximity to Eritrea and Sudan adds to its geopolitical significance.
